Hello again.

My interest in biomedical communications and product design began with a passion for people, a fascination with science and technology, and a desire to make a difference. Coming from a psychology and preclinical oncology research background, I took a big leap into medical illustration the moment I discovered it—primarily because I wanted to advance scientific knowledge and increase public health literacy on a broader and more creative platform. In grad school (Master of Science in Biomedical Communications), I learned more about community-centered design as a communication strategy, in addition to various visualization approaches. It has been interesting to combine my familiarity in the analytical scientific method with design thinking.

The experience only deepened my love for both science and art.

I strive to create profound and meaningful solutions that put the human element at the core. I believe effective and applicable changes can emerge by focusing on the user, and I've been fortunate to be able to connect the dots in my past for this purpose. Currently, I am especially interested in improving mental health, low-income healthcare, and public accessibility of scientific research.

When I'm not facing a screen, I enjoy reading non-technical books, trying DIY projects, staring into the ocean, swimming, running, hiking, and especially traveling (inspirations galore!).
