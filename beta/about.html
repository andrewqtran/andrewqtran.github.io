We R DNA n Art

That's my full name, spelled backwards. Aptly, my passions lie in healthcare, science, tech, and design. Coming from a psychology background and preclinical oncology research, I took a big leap into medical illustration the moment I discovered it. In grad school (Master of Science in Biomedical Communications), I learned more about community-centered design, in addition to various visualization approaches.

The experience only deepened my love for both science and art.

I strive to create impactful and meaningful solutions that put the human element at the core. I've been fortunate to be able to connect the dots in my past for this purpose. Currently, I am especially interested in problems in mental health, low-income health care, and accessibility of scientific research. I believe effective and applicable changes can emerge by focusing on the user.

When I'm not facing a screen, I enjoy reading non-technical books, trying DIY projects, staring into the ocean, swimming, running, hiking, and especially traveling (inspirations galore).
